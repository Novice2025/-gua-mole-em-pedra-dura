English Equivalents and Slang

While there's no direct, popular translation, several English sayings capture the same spirit of the proverb:

  • **"If at first you don't succeed, try, try again."** This is the most common and direct moral equivalent.
  • **"Slow and steady wins the race."** This phrase emphasizes the methodical, unhurried nature of the effort.
  • **"Where there's a will, there's a way."** This captures the determination aspect.
  • **"Persistence pays off."** A straightforward and modern way to express the same idea.
